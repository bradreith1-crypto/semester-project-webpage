Welcome to How To Ski for Beginners!

Part 1: Equipment

Your equipment is very important because faulty equipment can cause physical harm

  • if your clothing isnt warm enough, it can cause sickness such as hypothermia or a common cold
  • if your skis are too long or too short, you wont be able to control your direction correctly
  • if your boots are to big, they could slip off and take your skis with them
  • a helmet is optional, but without a good helmet, you could get major head injuries from a single crash into any solid object

Part 2: Know the Mountain

You need to understand the rules of the mountain and what the signs mean

  • greens mean they are the easiest on the mountain and a kindergartener could complete them
  • blues are a step above green and mean about a 5th grader could complete them
  • blacks are the hardest on the mountain and an adult or highly skilled teen could complete it

Part 3: Getting up the slopes

To get up to the top of the mountain you can use one of two methods.

  • the first way is the chair lift for getting high up on the mountain in comfort
  • the second way is the t-bar lift, which you grab on so it can pull you up the slopes
  • the third way isnt necessarily found all over the mountains, it is the magic carpet which is like a conveyor belt you stand on

Part 4: Getting down the slopes

The most important thing to learn to get down the mountain safely is how to stop and stay in control.

  • if you are a noob, you can position your skis in a pizza shape to slow down
  • the proper way is to position your skis parallel of eachother and turn from side to side, moving down the slope in a snakey manner
